Birthplace has always been essential to rap music. Compton, East Point, Queensbridge are just few of the many rap cradles made famous by their most gifted children. The rap fan is also a cartographer. But rap mapping is getting quite complex with time.

A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie grew up in the Bronx, not Atlanta or Toronto - nowadays it’s not common. But those who expect some sort of old school revival will soon be disappointed. His funky, cheesy synthesizer and carefree groove evoke more the Gold Coast (where the young boy spent some years on probations) than Gotham.

Florida, this is where Kodak Black comes from. Pompano Beach to be more precise. But you won’t find any sunny bass in his sound. He prefers a more sticky, sweaty trap : the kid is a fan of Lil Boosie, Baton Rouge’s finest.

On Drowning (Water)the two top-notch freshmen team up on a mournful melody, that looks like nothing else, except maybe that good old Scott Storch’s piano. 

Conclusion : it’s a whole new world out there.
