If you were a young movie-fan in the 90’s, with a peculiar taste for action flicks, then Shane Black was your man. From Lethal Weapon 2 to Last Action Hero, the genius screenwriter laid with stories made of moral codes, chivalrous attitudes, and cool punch lines, the foundation of the grown up I would become. I can’t say it went *that* well, but still.

Buddy films were Black’s signature. Movies about how to overcome differences, a much more satisfying spectacle, than, let’s say, big robots destroying random buildings. Recently his fantastic The Nice Guys proved that the genre was still relevant. Even more: necessary.

In Black’s filmography, The Last Boyscout remains my favorite movie to this date. It contains hundreds of cult dialogues. Let’s take this scene where Bruce Willis, playing Joe Hallenbeck, finds himself in a dark alley with a gun pointed at his head. The dialogue goes like this : 

Alley Thug: Wrong place, wrong time. Nothing personal.

Joe Hallenbeck: That’s what you think. Last night I fucked your wife.

Alley Thug: Oh you did, hah? How’d you know it was my wife?

Joe Hallenbeck: She said her husband was a big pimp lookin’ motherfucker with a hat.

Alley Thug: Oh, you’re real cool for somebody who’s about to take a bullet.

Joe Hallenbeck: After fucking your wife I’ll take two.

The joke kills the thug. Literally.

To get out of a dangerous situation in a buddy film, it’s all about the wit. You need to talk slick, and talk fast.

According to Dan Charnas, this performance is the very definition of rapping. No wonder rap music makes such a fitting format for perpetuating buddy films spirit. Outkast, Starlito & Don Trip, Run the Jewels, exemples of duos working on this duo dynamic are numerous.

Last year, coming straight from Bruxelles, Caballero & JeanJass offered with Double Hélice their own successful equation. Its sequel is now eagerly awaited.

Their last release is a classical ego trip (and a tribute to Birdman) that foreshadows a Double Hélice 2 even better than the first one.

As every 90’s action flick fans know, the second episode is always the best.
