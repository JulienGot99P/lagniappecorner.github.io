An unknown, inhabited planet hosting the memory of a lost civilization. A starry sky, enchanting gate to another dimension. The setting of The Weeknd’s last clip is a pure tribute to 80’s science fiction. No wonder, considering Daft Punk is featured on the single.

5 years ago, to the day, Jean Giraud passed away. The comic book artist, also known as Moebius, left behind a collection of masterpieces (l’Incal, Arzach) that defined the iconography used in the video. 

Not only was Moebius a genius of comic books, but his contribution to the big screen, as screenwriter and designer, is enormous. In 1981, coming out of his work on Alien, he designed the unforgettable set and costumes for the movie Tron : 30 years later, Daft Punk will produce a thrilling soundtrack for the (poor) remake.  

To celebrate the mastermind, I listed below three movies that every starboy should watch. 

The Time Masters (1982)

A film directed by René Laloux and designed by Mœbius. It’s the story of a young boy, lost on a desert planet full of giant hornets, and the journey of the space pilot Jaffar to save him. It’s charming and disturbing at the same time : everything an animation movie should be.    

Heavy Metal (1981)

Moebius was famous for his work in the French cult magazine Metal Hurlant, first issued in 1975 (the magazine later got an American edition, Heavy Metal).

In 1981, Heavy Metal : The Movie gathered a collection of short-stories influenced by the magazine. Even if Moebius wasn’t involved in the production, the last story Taarna is directly inspired by his comic book Arzach. The result is flawed, but has a unique madness.  

Jordorowski’s Dune (2013)

It was a legend going on the internet. The tale of a time when Star Wars didn’t even exist yet : a team of brilliant minds once tried to adapt on screen the greatest book of science fiction, Frank Herbert’s Dune. Jodorowsky, H.R. Giger, Moebius, Chris Foss and Dan O’bannon were about to make the paradigm of all sci-fi movies, but Hollywood studios junked it up. The movie never came on screen, but its legacy changed the face of cinema.

Jordorowski’s Dune is the documentary that explores why the disaster was in reality a miracle.
